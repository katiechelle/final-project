  1. Bachelor and Bachelorette Parties
    • thrown by wedding party
    • typically seperate parties for bride and groom but can be together
    • "last fling before the ring"
    • can be anywhere from 1 day to 1 week long
  2. Rehersal Dinner
    • typically the night before the wedding
    • wedding party and other guests who are participating in the ceremony are invited
    • reherse walking down aisle, walking up the aisle, and other important cues
    • formal dinner to follow
  3. Ceremony
    • civil ceremonies occur at churches or just regular wedding venues
    • bride and bridesmaids get ready seperate from the groom and groomsmen
    • when wedding starts, the officiant will be the first one down the aisle
    • following the officiant, the order goes groom, bridal party, and finally the bride
    • the bride and groom will exchange vows and rings under the direction of the officiant
    • ceremonies last anywhere from 5 minutes to 30 minutes on average
    • after being pronounced husband and wife, the couple kisses and then recesses
  4. Cocktail Hour
    • this is time for the bride, groom, and family to take photos
    • guests will drink and play games in a designated area
    • sometimes the bride and groom join cocktail hour if they finish pictures quickly
    • typically only lasts an hour but can go as long as an hour and a half depending on the couple
  5. Reception
    • the party aspect of a wedding
    • usually starts with a dinner
    • includes lot of iconic traditions such as toasts, bouquet toss, garter toss, first dance, mother son dance, father daughter dance, and other cultural traditions
    • normally ends with an hour or so of an open dance floor
    • couple leaves in a grand exit
